# Sources — Phase 1 research

Authoritative public references used to ground the partner brief. Retrieved
mid-2026. Figures are cited in-line in `partner-brief.md` with their reporting
period. This file is the reference list and a map of what each source contributes.

## Regulator / government — market and financial data
1. **APRA — Quarterly private health insurance performance statistics.**
   https://www.apra.gov.au/quarterly-private-health-insurance-performance-statistics
   → Industry revenue, insurance service result, net profit, capital, benefits paid.
   Latest cited: December 2025 quarter release.
   https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-quarterly-insurance-statistics-december-2025
2. **APRA — Annual private health insurance statistics (2024–25).**
   https://www.apra.gov.au/news-and-publications/apra-releases-annual-private-health-insurance-statistics-2024-2025
   → Membership, coverage, per-fund benefit ratios and management-expense ratios.
3. **APRA — Quarterly membership and benefits summary (Sept 2025).**
   https://www.apra.gov.au/quarterly-private-health-insurance-membership-and-benefits-summary-september-2025
   → Participation and benefits trend series.
4. **Dept of Health & Aged Care — Private Hospital Sector Financial Health Check
   (summary, Oct/Nov 2024).**
   https://www.health.gov.au/resources/collections/private-hospital-sector-financial-health-check-resources
   Minister's release: https://www.health.gov.au/ministers/the-hon-mark-butler-mp/media/private-hospital-sector-financial-health-check
   → 647 private hospitals / 36,000+ beds (July 2024); data from 243 hospitals (58% of
   separations, 63% of revenue); profit margins 5.1% (2020–21) → −0.1% (2023–24);
   private hospitals deliver >40% of admissions and ~70% of elective surgery.
5. **Commonwealth Ombudsman (PHIO) — State of the Health Funds Report (2023–24).**
   https://www.ombudsman.gov.au/publications/reports/state-of-the-health-funds
   → Per-fund complaints, benefit ratios, % of premiums returned as benefits.
6. **AIHW — hospital activity / admitted patient care & MyHospitals.**
   https://www.aihw.gov.au/  → separations, same-day/day-surgery growth, case mix.
7. **ACCC — Private health insurance report to the Senate (2023–24).**
   https://www.accc.gov.au/system/files/private-health-insurance-report-2023-24.pdf
   → Consumer-side and premium-round context.

## Competition / buying groups (structural power imbalance)
8. **ACCC — Honeysuckle Health and nib buying group authorisation.**
   https://www.accc.gov.au/media-release/honeysuckle-health-nib-buying-group-authorised-with-condition
   Register: https://www.accc.gov.au/public-registers/authorisations-and-notifications-registers/authorisations-register/honeysuckle-health-and-nib
   → 5-year authorisation; condition excluding the majors; 40% state cap; AHSA (27
   insurers) and ARHG (4 insurers) context.
9. **ACCC — Small business collective bargaining class exemption (in force 2021).**
   https://www.accc.gov.au/business/collective-bargaining-and-boycotts/collective-bargaining-class-exemption
   → Legal channel letting eligible small businesses (incl. small day hospitals)
   collectively negotiate without individual authorisation. NB competition-law line:
   this permits collective *bargaining with a counterparty*, NOT price coordination
   between competing hospitals.

10. **Australian Private Hospitals Association (APHA) — Private Hospital Facts.**
    https://www.apha.org.au/private-hospital-facts.html
11. **Day Hospitals Australia — sector data & advocacy ("Fork in the Road" report).**
    https://www.dayhospitalsaustralia.net.au/
12. **Healthscope — contract terminations (Bupa, AHSA) and 2025 receivership** (sector
    news, multiple outlets incl. HospitalHealth, iSelect):
    https://www.hospitalhealth.com.au/content/facility-admin/news/healthscope-terminates-bupa-ahsa-contracts-1717310579

## Legislation / rules (Contract Agent grounding + BATNA)
13. **Private Health Insurance Act 2007** and subordinate rules — PHI (Benefit
    Requirements) Rules, PHI (Accreditation) Rules. https://www.legislation.gov.au/
14. **Dept of Health — Second-tier default benefits (guidelines & eligibility).**
    https://www.health.gov.au/topics/private-health-insurance/operating-rules-for-private-health-insurers-and-providers/become-a-second-tier-default-benefits-eligible-hospital
    → ≥85% of the average charge under the insurer's agreements for comparable
    hospitals in the same state/category — the structural fallback / BATNA.
15. **Informed financial consent** obligations (Dept of Health guidance).
    https://www.health.gov.au/topics/private-health-insurance/what-private-health-insurance-covers/out-of-pocket-costs
