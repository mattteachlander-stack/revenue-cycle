# Bayview Day Surgery — facility profile

> **SYNTHETIC DATA.** Fictional facility created for the prototype. Figures are
> invented, loosely shaped by public sector aggregates so they read realistically.

| | |
|---|---|
| Facility | Bayview Day Surgery Pty Ltd |

| Theatres | 4 (plus 2 procedure rooms) |

Bayview is typical of its segment: clinically strong, financially thin, and run by an
executive team of two (CEO + finance/office manager) with no contracting function.
HPPA renewals are handled personally by the CEO, on top of the day job.

Quality indicators: unplanned transfer rate 0.21%, unplanned return-to-theatre 0.06%,
same-day discharge failure 0.4% — all better than the synthetic state benchmark used
in the demo. This clinical record is a negotiation asset the current contract does not
monetise.

AusCare concentration is the strategic exposure: **$3.12m of annual revenue** rides on
the agreement expiring 30 November 2026. At second-tier default, modelling puts the
same activity at ~$2.65m — a **~$470k (15%) downside**, which is most of the
facility's EBITDA. That asymmetry is exactly what the fund's contracting team can see
and the CEO currently cannot.

## AusCare relationship detail (the negotiation at hand)

- Episodes: 1,690/yr (gastro 780, ophthalmology 640, ortho 160, plastics 110)
- Revenue: $3.12m/yr; average revenue per AusCare episode $1,846
- Rate position (vs the demo's synthetic state medians): cataract Band 2 **−7.2%**,
  colonoscopy Band 1 **−3.8%**, arthroscopy Band 3 **−5.5%**, plastics **at market**
- Contract: signed 1 Dec 2023, 3-year term, expires 30 Nov 2026; negotiation window
  opens now (fund's paper says proposals due by 31 Aug 2026)
- History: FY25 indexation paid at 1.9% against CPI 3.4% via the cl. 12.3 carve-out;
  two re-banding notices in FY26 moved 4 ophthalmic item groups from Band 2 → Band 1
- Growth plan: theatre 4 currently at 61% utilisation; ortho pipeline could add
  ~420 AusCare-insured episodes/yr — leverage the current agreement doesn't price
