## Part A — Preliminary

### 1. Parties and purpose
1.1 This Agreement is a hospital purchaser–provider agreement for the purposes of the
*Private Health Insurance Act 2007* (Cth) under which the Fund agrees to pay benefits
for Hospital Treatment provided by the Hospital to Eligible Policyholders.

1.2 The Hospital is a licensed day procedure centre. No benefits are payable under
this Agreement for overnight accommodation except as expressly provided in clause 9.

### 2. Term
2.1 This Agreement commences on 1 December 2023 and, subject to earlier termination
under clause 19, expires on 30 November 2026 ("**the Term**").

2.2 If negotiations for a replacement agreement are not concluded at expiry, this
Agreement continues on a month-to-month basis on the rates current at expiry, and
**no indexation applies during any holdover period.**

### 3. Definitions (extract)
- "**Banding Schedule**" means Schedule 1 as amended from time to time in accordance
  with clause 8.
- "**Case Payment**" means the single bundled amount payable per Episode as set out
  in the Banding Schedule, inclusive of all facility fees, theatre fees, bed fees,
  nursing, non-PBS pharmaceuticals administered on the day, and all consumables other
  than Listed Prostheses to the extent provided by clause 10.
- "**CPI**" means the All Groups Consumer Price Index (weighted average of eight
  capital cities) published by the Australian Bureau of Statistics.
- "**Eligible Policyholder**" means a person insured under a complying health
  insurance policy issued by the Fund whose cover includes the relevant treatment.
- "**Episode**" means a single same-day admitted episode of Hospital Treatment.
- "**Recognised Procedure**" means a procedure identified by MBS item number and
  assigned to a Band in the Banding Schedule.

## Part B — Services and payment

### 4. Services
4.1 The Hospital may provide, and the Fund will pay for, Recognised Procedures in the
specialty groups of gastroenterology, ophthalmology, orthopaedics (day surgical),
and plastic & reconstructive surgery (day surgical), as set out in the Banding
Schedule.

4.2 New MBS item numbers introduced during the Term are payable only once assigned to
a Band by the Fund, which the Fund will do within 60 business days of a written
request by the Hospital.

### 5. Eligibility verification and certification
5.1 The Hospital must verify each patient's eligibility through the Fund's online
eligibility system (OEC) prior to admission, and must obtain the patient's informed
financial consent in accordance with applicable law and industry codes.

### 6. Payment structure
6.1 All Episodes are remunerated by **Case Payment** in accordance with the Banding
Schedule. There are no per-diem payments under this Agreement.

6.2 The Case Payment is payment in full for the Episode. The Hospital must not
charge an Eligible Policyholder any amount for services forming part of the Episode,
other than amounts disclosed and agreed through informed financial consent for
services excluded by Schedule 2.

### 7. Rates
7.1 The rates payable are those in the Banding Schedule (Schedule 1), as indexed
under clause 12 and as amended under clause 8.

### 8. Banding
8.1 Each Recognised Procedure is assigned to one Band. The Case Payment for an
Episode is the rate for the highest Band procedure performed in that Episode; where
two or more Recognised Procedures are performed, the second and subsequent
procedures attract no additional payment except as noted in the Banding Schedule.

8.2 The Fund will review the Banding Schedule annually having regard to MBS changes,
clinical practice, and utilisation data.

8.3 The Hospital may request re-banding of a Recognised Procedure by written
submission with supporting clinical and cost data. The Fund will respond within 90
business days.

8.4 **The Fund may reassign any Recognised Procedure to a different Band by giving
the Hospital 30 days' written notice, where the Fund reasonably considers the
reassignment reflects the procedure's clinical complexity, resource intensity, or
prevailing market classification. A reassignment under this clause is not a
variation for the purposes of clause 21 and does not require the Hospital's
consent.**

### 9. Unplanned admissions and transfers
9.1 Where a patient cannot be safely discharged on the day of a procedure and is
transferred to an overnight facility, the Fund pays the Hospital the applicable Case
Payment and pays the receiving facility under its own arrangements. No additional
amount is payable to the Hospital for extended recovery prior to transfer.

### 10. Prostheses and high-cost consumables
10.1 Listed Prostheses (as defined in the Prescribed List arrangements) are payable
by the Fund at the Prescribed List benefit, in addition to the Case Payment, except
that:

10.2 **For Band 2 ophthalmic procedures, the Case Payment is inclusive of the
intraocular lens and all associated consumables, and no separate prosthesis benefit
is payable, regardless of the lens selected.**

10.3 Non-listed consumables, single-use instruments, and equipment charges are
included in the Case Payment and are not separately billable to the Fund or the
patient.

### 11. GST
11.1 Amounts are exclusive of GST, which is payable in addition where applicable.

### 12. Indexation
12.1 On each anniversary of the Commencement Date, the rates in the Banding Schedule
will be adjusted by the percentage movement in CPI over the 12 months to the most
recent June quarter ("**the Indexation Amount**").

12.2 The Indexation Amount is capped at 4.0% in any year.

12.3 **Notwithstanding clauses 12.1 and 12.2, the Fund may, by notice before an
anniversary, apply a lower Indexation Amount (including zero) where the Fund
determines, in its discretion, that the outcome of the annual premium round, claims
inflation, or portfolio affordability so requires. The Fund will act reasonably in
making such a determination and will provide the Hospital with a written summary of
its grounds.**

12.4 Indexation does not apply to any rate first agreed within 6 months before the
relevant anniversary.

## Part C — Claims, audit and information

### 13. Claiming
13.1 Claims must be submitted electronically via the ECLIPSE system with certified
episode coding.

### 14. Payment, adjustment and recovery
14.1 The Fund will pay clean claims within 15 business days of receipt.

14.2 **A claim must be lodged within 30 days of the date of discharge. The Fund may
reject a claim lodged after that period, and the Hospital must not recover the
shortfall from the patient.**

14.3 The Fund may adjust a claim that contains a manifest error and will notify the
Hospital of the adjustment and its grounds.

14.4 The Hospital may dispute an adjustment within 20 business days under clause 18.

14.5 **Where the Fund determines that an amount has been overpaid (including
following a re-banding under clause 8.4 taking effect, a coding review, or an audit
under clause 15), the Fund may recover the overpayment at any time within 24 months
of payment, including by set-off against any amount otherwise payable to the
Hospital, without prejudice to its other remedies.**

### 15. Audit
15.1 The Fund may audit the Hospital's claims, coding, and clinical records relevant
to Episodes on 10 business days' notice, no more than twice per year absent
reasonable suspicion of systematic error.

15.2 The Hospital bears its own costs of an audit. Where an audit identifies a net
overpayment exceeding 2% of the audited sample value, the Hospital must also pay the
Fund's reasonable audit costs.

### 16. Data and reporting
16.1 The Hospital must provide the Fund with HCP (Hospital Casemix Protocol) data
and such utilisation reports as the Fund reasonably requires, monthly.

16.2 Data provided under this clause may be used by the Fund for benchmarking,
contracting, and portfolio analysis.

## Part D — Quality, compliance and relationship

### 17. Quality and safety
17.1 The Hospital must maintain NSQHS accreditation, its state licence, and
insurance (professional indemnity $20m, public liability $20m per occurrence).

17.2 The Hospital will report the KPI set in Schedule 3 (unplanned transfer rate,
unplanned return to theatre, same-day discharge failure, SAB rate) quarterly.

17.3 Persistent adverse variance against Schedule 3 benchmarks, unremedied for two
consecutive quarters after notice, is a material breach for the purposes of clause
19.2. **No payment, bonus, or rate adjustment attaches to favourable KPI
performance.**

### 18. Dispute resolution
18.1 The parties will first negotiate in good faith at CEO/executive level for 20
business days.

18.2 Failing resolution, either party may refer the dispute to mediation
administered by the Resolution Institute (Melbourne) before commencing proceedings,
except for urgent injunctive relief.

18.3 The parties note the availability of the second-tier default benefit framework
and of external avenues including the Commonwealth Ombudsman for consumer matters.

### 19. Termination
19.1 **The Fund may terminate this Agreement without cause by giving the Hospital 90
days' written notice. The Hospital may terminate this Agreement without cause by
giving the Fund 180 days' written notice.**

19.2 Either party may terminate for material breach not remedied within 30 days of
notice, or immediately on the other party's insolvency or loss of licence,
registration, or (in the Hospital's case) accreditation.

19.3 On termination or expiry, benefits for Episodes with a procedure date after the
termination date are payable at the rates and under the conditions applicable under
the *Private Health Insurance (Benefit Requirements) Rules* to a facility without an
agreement (including any applicable second-tier default benefit), and not under this
Agreement.

### 20. Confidentiality
20.1 The terms of this Agreement, including all rates, are confidential and must not
be disclosed except to professional advisers, as required by law, or with consent.
**The Hospital must not disclose the rates in Schedule 1 to any other hospital or
day procedure centre, or to any person acting for one.**

### 21. Variation, assignment, notices, law
21.1 Except as provided by clauses 8.4 and 12.3, this Agreement may be varied only
in writing signed by both parties.

21.2 The Hospital may not assign this Agreement (including on a sale of business)
without the Fund's consent, not to be unreasonably withheld.

21.3 Governing law: Victoria.

## Schedule 2 — Exclusions (extract)

No benefit is payable under this Agreement for: cosmetic procedures without MBS
item; bariatric procedures; IVF/ART procedures; dental procedures other than
MBS-listed oral surgical items; hyperbaric therapy; any procedure performed by a
practitioner without current credentialing at the Hospital; experimental or trial
procedures. Excluded services may be provided on a self-funded basis with informed
financial consent.
