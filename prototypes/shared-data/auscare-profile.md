## What the numbers mean at the negotiation table

- **Headroom exists.** A benefit ratio of 83.1% is *below* the industry band — AusCare
  returns less of each premium dollar as benefits than the average fund, while its MER
  runs *above* the industry norm. Rate discipline with providers is subsidising both
  margin and overhead. This is a documented leverage point, not a grievance: the fund
  approved a 3.85% premium increase while paying Bayview 1.9% indexation.
- **Bayview is small to AusCare, but not free.** Bayview's $3.12m is ~0.3% of AusCare's
  hospital benefits — the fund can absorb a walk-away. But AusCare's members in
  Bayview's catchment would lose the area's shortest ophthalmic and endoscopy wait
  times, and second-tier default still obliges AusCare to pay ~85% of comparable rates
  while its members face gap uncertainty and complaint risk (where AusCare already
  over-indexes).
- **Known contracting behaviour** *(synthetic pattern, typical of the segment)*:
  opens with CPI-minus offers citing "affordability"; uses re-banding as a quiet
  rate cut; long response cycles (time favours the incumbent contract); prefers
  bilateral confidentiality clauses; historically settles at or near CPI with
  selected band corrections when a facility shows credible walk-away modelling and
  clinical-quality data.

## Fund-side negotiation team (who the CEO is actually facing)

Provider Contracting — Southern Region: a national manager, two contract managers,
and an analytics function with visibility of every comparable facility's negotiated
rates in Victoria. Standard playbook, template wording, no emotional stake, and no
deadline pressure. This asymmetry — not any individual's conduct — is the problem the
copilot exists to correct.
